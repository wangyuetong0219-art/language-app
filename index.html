Why Do We Dream?
Scientists have long been fascinated by dreams. Every night, while we sleep, our brains create vivid stories, emotions, and images.

One popular theory suggests that dreams help us process emotions and consolidate memories. During sleep, the brain replays experiences from the day, deciding what to keep and what to discard.

Another idea is that dreams help us rehearse responses to challenging situations — which might explain why many people dream about exams or public speaking.
